CARP Posters



Girl's Skate Clinic Poster

I made this poster. It's about a girls skate clinic. I had to use the Carp Principles. Contrast, alignment, repitition, proximity. For the finished poster, I applied contrast by using black and white for the background and text. In terms of alignment, I made sure all the sides of the text were aligned on the same line. When it came to repitition, I used repeated a lot of symbols and also kept the font and colour scheme the same for each section. Lastly for the proximity part I made sure all of the text were the same difference from each other. After a discussion with my tutor I was advised to choose my middle layout. She also gave me some advice on how to improve my poster in terms of it's proximity as the texts were not in clear sections.

For the second layout I decided to lay it out vertically into 3 sections, the main one's being the image and information. I incorporated the contrast through the colours of the different sections, as well as through the contrast of the text and sections. In terms of proximity, I made sure all the information was placed with the same amount of distance between them, whereas in terms of alignment, I made sure they all were aligned with the sides of the image box. I decided to incorporate repitition by using the same colours and font, as well as breaking up the the information into a few chunks.

The third layout I did used the colours dark brown and white to showcase the contrast element of CARP. When it came to the alignment, I chose to line up the sides of the information and the logo so they were all aligned. I divided the information into 4 chunks and placed 2 of each at different parts of the poster,this represented repition. I applied the proximity element of CARP by making sure the size of the spaces in between the sections were the same.

Typography Posters


Transitional

This is a poster I made following the transitional typographic system. The process of designing this poster was slightly more time consuming as I had to use the type on path tool. After I played around with and tried a few different styles and positions, I was able to produce this poster. Though it took more time, the result was pretty satisfying to see.

Dilatational

This is a poster I made following the dilatational typographic system. For this typographic system I had to ensure that my text was placed in a fluid and circle type manner. I also had to use the type on path tool for this poster. Before designing I had to refer to some examples online to ensure that I was carrying out this typographic system in the correct manner.

Modular

This a poster I made following the modular typographic system. The concept of the modular tpographic system was slightly confusing at first as it's quite similar and heavily related to the grid typographic system. After looking at a few posters and reading, I understood that the text had to be placed in spaces or shapes. The end result for this poster turned out a lot neater compared to my other posters.

Grid

This a poster I made following the grid typographic system. As mentioned, the similarity between the grid and modular typographic system was slightly confusing. After I finished my initial design, my tutor advised me to tweak it a bit as it wasn't clearly representing the grid typographic system. The poster I created was following both modular and grid systems. After changing some parts of the design I was able to produce this poster.

Random

This a poster I made following the random typographic system. This typographic system required us to really let our creativity flow and think outside the box. Our tutor repeatedly mentioned that she would advise to us make our posters as random as possible. I think the process of making this poster was quite fun since we had no rules and requirements to follow. After showing my tutors my poster they told me to try and make it even more random, so that's what I tried to do.

Radial

This a poster I made following the radial typographic system. For this type system, we had to make sure that the text was designed in a way which it resembled sun rays coming out from the sun. This was one of the systems where I had to look for inspiration and information online to ensure I was designing correctly. I used an orange and black colour scheme to represent the sunray concept. I was pretty happy with the result for this poster, though it's pretty simple.

Bilateral

This a poster I made following the bilateral typographic system. The concept of the bilateral typographic system was pretty easy to understand as it basically just meant that the text had to be centered in the middle of the design. Suprisingly though it turned out to be quite challenging to design as I had to come up with a way for it to not seem boring. It would have been easy to ust put the text going straight down, but I had to come up with a way to make the design more interesting.

Axial

This a poster I made following the axial typographic system. The axial system was the first poster that I started working on and now from just looking at it I can see that it looks slightly busier than the others. Maybe it was the fact that I used different tones of red for the poster. I also had to look at some references for the system just so I could get a better understanding of how it's usually designed.

Reflection Essay

In this section, I'm going to be talking about my experience making this website, including the challenges I faced and what I would improve if I were to repeat the process. We were given quite a long period of time for this assignment but at the same time we were also still being introduced to graphic and web design so it wasn't really possible for us to start straight away. The initial process of being introduced to web design was rather nerve-wracking as there were lots of new terms and software that I'd never heard of being used. Honestly, getting familiar with using GitHub and having to make sure that my files were properly linked for each worksheet took up a lot more time than I'd like to admit. The process of constantly being faced with technical issues and then eventually figuring them out has made my understanding of things better now. Though there are still a lot of things I'm unsure of, that process allowed me to get the hang of things. If I were to repeat this process, I probably would have been more proactive when it came to learning how to code and design websites. I probably could have used my free time to gain more knowledge so I could have excelled more for this project but I would still say that I'm proud of what I've accomplished as this is my first attempt at creating a website.

The process of creating the typography and CARP posters went a lot more smoothly compared to designing this website as I felt like my creativity was able to flow better. Though graphic design also requires us to use new software, it wasn't too difficult to bring my ideas to life as the process was less complicated. Before coming to university, I did do some graphic design just as a hobby, so it didn't feel as intimidating as web design. There were times where I had trouble using the software but I was able to find solutions for my issues by asking my tutors or searching for them online.

One thing that I've observed is that the process of designing things using code is a lot different, and it requires you to think more. I realised that this was a subject that needs us to learn how to balance both our creative and practical sides. The fact that we were asked to complete coding worksheets definitely made the process of making this website a lot easier. Through completing them, the terms, and concepts of web design started to become more familiar and understandable. Additionally, having the coding worksheets to refer to helped to refresh my memory on how to carry out certain processes. In regards to the process of this entire assignment as a whole, I probably would have tried to manage my time more efficiently so I would have had more time to improve and refine my website and posters before the deadline. One aspect that I think I did well was asking for help and advice. I frequently made sure to ask my tutors for advice on how to improve my designs and how to solve coding issues. I feel as though this allowed me to improve the quality of my work and ensure I was on the right track for this assessment.
